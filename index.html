About

Palm Coast Publishing was founded in 2003 with a simple goal: create mathematics texts that feel like a conversation with a careful teacher rather than a wall of symbols. Our titles emphasize worked examples, scaffolded problem sets, and a steady progression from intuition to formalism.

The visual identity of the site is anchored by an original photograph taken in Egypt, reflecting our connection to the long history of mathematics while we continue to build modern tools for today’s learners.

Differential Equations

An Introduction to Differential Equations introduces first‑order equations, linear equations of higher order, systems of differential equations, and qualitative methods. Applications are drawn from physics, engineering, and the life sciences, with an emphasis on modeling and interpretation.

Each chapter includes a progression from guided examples to more open‑ended problems, making the book suitable for both lecture‑based and inquiry‑oriented courses.

A Student's Approach to Precalculus

A Student's Approach to Precalculus is designed as a true bridge to calculus, not a repetition of Algebra II. Functions are treated as central objects, with attention to multiple representations, transformations, and composition.

Trigonometry, analytic geometry, and introductory limits are developed in a way that prepares students for the conceptual and notational demands of a first calculus course.

Using the TI‑83

This guide helps students and instructors use the TI‑83 as a tool for exploration rather than a black box. Topics include graphing functions, numerical root‑finding, regression, and basic statistics.

Screen captures and keystroke‑level instructions are paired with conceptual commentary so that technology supports, rather than replaces, mathematical thinking.

Algebra II For You

Algebra II For You is written with the student’s voice in mind. Each section begins with a short, plain‑language overview before moving into formal definitions and worked examples.

The text covers polynomial, rational, exponential, and logarithmic functions, sequences and series, and introductory probability, with abundant practice at multiple levels of difficulty.

Math Mine

Math Mine is a Python‑based content engine that generates curriculum‑aligned mathematics worksheets and problem sets on demand. It is designed as a modular, backend‑driven system with clear service boundaries and idempotent job processing.

Within Palm Coast Publishing, Math Mine powers supplemental materials for our texts—allowing instructors to create fresh practice sets, targeted review sheets, and variations of core problems while preserving the pedagogical structure of the books.

At a high level, Math Mine consists of an API layer, an asynchronous worker queue, and a plug‑in architecture for topic‑specific generators, making it straightforward to extend from algebra and precalculus into more advanced subjects.
